Google Accessibility Blog

Updates from the Google Accessibility team

Latest news and updates

  • Voice Access for Android now available

    Voice Access is an accessibility service for Android that lets you use your Android device without touching the screen. Voice Access was in beta and is now publicly available on Google Play. Read on for more details.

    Android Wednesday, September 26, 2018 Google Accessibility Team

  • Google Accessibility at ACB and NFB 2018

    The American Council of the Blind (ACB) and National Federation of the Blind (NFB) recently held their 2018 annual conferences where the Google Accessibility team announced some new product features and improvements. Read more for a list of announcements and Blind Bargains podcast interviews with members of the Google Accessibility team.

    Events Wednesday, September 26, 2018 Google Accessibility Team

  • Participate in an online accessibility research study

    Do you use an iPhone or Android smartphone with the Google Maps app installed? We're looking for feedback in a one hour online accessibility research study. Take our survey to find out if you’re a match. Those selected will get a token of our appreciation.

    Announcements Thursday, August 23, 2018 Google Accessibility Team

  • Streaming support spec for hearing aids on Android

    Hearing aid companies now have a way to make their devices available to hearing aid users on Android.

    Android Thursday, August 16, 2018 Google Accessibility Team

  • Chat with the Google Disability Support team

    The Disability Support team is available to answer questions about using assistive technology with Google products and accessibility features and functionalities within Google products. You can now contact the team through chat support, or send an email and receive a response within 24 business hours. Read more to learn how the team can help you and ways to get in touch with them today.

    Announcements Friday, August 03, 2018 Google Accessibility Team
